Better Than Standard?

Mia and Pavel each have what appears to be a standard six-sided die with 1+2+3+4+5+6=211 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + 6 = 211+2+3+4+5+6=21 dots, or pips, on its faces, and are playing a game. They each roll their die, and whoever gets the higher number wins. If they roll the same number, it's a tie and they roll again.

Mia's die is more than it seems, though. The pips on her die are stickers that she can peel off and reposition however she wants. She moves the dots around on the faces of her die, and now her die has faces with 3,3,3,4,4,3, 3, 3, 4, 4,3,3,3,4,4, and 444 pips.

Did Mia improve her chances of winning, or not? Keep reading to find out, or jump ahead to today's challenge if you feel ready.

If Mia and Pavel each roll a standard die, Mia can't win by rolling a 1;1;1; the best she can do is to tie and roll again. If they both came from the 666 face, then that face will now lose to a 555 or a 6.6.6.

    Let's look at the face with the 3 on it. It will win against 1 and 2, lose against 4,5, and 6, and draw against another 3. Let's call a win a +1, a loss a -1 and a draw a 0. We can then give the side a score based on the chance of a win, draw or loss. The three scores -1 because it wins agains 2 faces, loses against three and draws against one and 2-3+0=-1.

    In fact the faces one to six score -5, -3, -1, 1, 3, and 5. We can add up the score of all of out faces and get a score for the die. That score, not too surprisingly, is 0. Mia will win as often as Pavel does because they are, initially using the dame design of dice.

    If we move a dot from one face to another we raise the score of one face at the expense of the other. But what if we can get that increase to be asymmetrical? Well... not if we only use the numbers one to six. For every pip that I lose from a face that face loses 2 from its score. For every pip I add to a face that face gains 2.

    So let's look at how the numbers zero and seven fair. Zero loses against everything so it has a score of -6 and seven wins against everything so it has a score of 6. These don't fit into the same pattern as the other numbers because they can't draw against another number: they can only win or lose.

    If Mia takes the pip off the one side, their die loses 1 from its score. If they put that pip onto 2, 3, 4, or 5 that face (and therefore the die) gains 2 so, by moving the pip from the one to 2, 3, 4, or 5 they can improve their die and win more against their opponent.

    It's enough to look at the last operand, 1.

    Multiplying or dividing by 1 doesn't change the parity, so half of the equations have the same parity as the rest of the expression (i.e. the result we'd get the 1 were removed).

    Adding or subtracting by 1 always flips parity, so the other half of the equations have the opposite parity as the rest of the expression. (Note: Due to order of operations, this is always performed last)

    This already shows us that the total parity must balance out between even and odd, no matter what the rest of the expression looks like.

    import itertools

def to_str(number_list, operation_list):
    assert len(number_list) == len(operation_list)+1
    piece_list = [str(num)+op for (num, op) in itertools.zip_longest(number_list,
                                                                     operation_list,
                                                                     fillvalue='')]
    return ''.join(piece_list)

OPERATION_LIST = ['*', '+', '-', '/']
NUMBER_LIST = [7, 5, 3, 1]
SOLUTION_DATA = {'odd': [], 'even': []}
for cur_oper_list in itertools.product(OPERATION_LIST, repeat=len(NUMBER_LIST)-1):

    cur_val = eval(cur_str)
    if cur_val%2 == 0:
        SOLUTION_DATA['even'].append(f"{cur_str} = {cur_val}")

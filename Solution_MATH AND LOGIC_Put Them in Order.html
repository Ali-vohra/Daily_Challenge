Put Them in Order

How much information do you need to put three things in order, like the finish order of a three-person race? It turns out, not much at all.

Suppose that, in a race among Amelia, Becca, and Carlos, we only know one fact:

Amelia and Becca both finished within one place of Carlos.

What are the possible orders for the entire race? Keep reading to see some reasoning we can use on order puzzles like these, or jump ahead to today's challenge for a trickier problem.

We can often make order problems like these easier by focusing on a single unknown factor and then testing different cases for that unknown. For example, in this problem, we can focus on "where did Becca finish?" and then test different cases to see if they conflict with any facts.

The fact Amelia and Becca both finished within one place of Carlos might not seem to directly tell us anything about the runners' finish order, but it does help us narrow down the possible finish orders. To see this logic demonstrated, use the arrows below the figure:

Carlos must be between Becca and Amelia, so Becca's possible finishing positions completely determine the finishing order of the race. There are two possible total finishing orders: one where Becca is first, and one where Becca is last.

What if we add another runner to the race, Dara, and have a more complicated scenario?

  • Only Amelia finished within one place of Dara.

  • Becca finished two places from Amelia.

  • Dara did not finish last.

But how do you know that this is the right first step in the solution? The first step is the hardest. So let's say why this is the best first step. Main reason:

Letters in each-

  • A) Truck & Barge - 5 Letters
  • B) Tugboat - 7 Letters:
  • C) Unicycle - 8 Letters

Now, Let's solve each slot-

  • 1) Ferry is fixed.
  • 2) As ferry is 5 letters, only B or C can come. But only C shares a letter so Unicycle is next.
  • 3) If we use B, both of A (5 letters) will have to come in next two slots which is not allowed, so B can't go here. Either Truck or Barge (A) can come here (both share a letter).
  • 4) Our only option now is Tugboat here (B).
  • 5) Either of A can come here now (both share a letter). So either Truck or Barge.
  • 6) Cab is fixed (3 Letters).

So we have two options in (3) slot - Truck or Barge, and whichever remains comes in (5) slot. Hence, answer is two.

Tilted Square

What is the side length of the colored square?

Keep reading for some reasoning we can use to help to answer this question, or skip straight to the challenge below.

While it may look like there are only four right triangles around the colored square, there are actually twelve of them. For each side of the square, there are three right triangles with a side on that side of the square. Let's look at the three that have a side on the top side of the square, and see how they're related.

All three triangles have the same angle measures, so they are all similar. We can see that triangle A's legs are in a 333 to 111 ratio, so the same must be true for triangles B and C. Let's use this fact to label triangle B's legs.

We have all three side lengths expressed, and applying the Pythagorean theorem gives us

(3a)2+a2=329a2+a2=910a2=9a2=910a=910a=310. \begin{aligned} (3a)^2 + a^2 & = 3^2 \\ 9a^2 + a^2 & = 9 \\ 10a^2 & = 9 \\ a^2 &= \frac{9}{10} \\ a & = \sqrt{\frac{9}{10}} \\ a & = \frac{3}{\sqrt{10}}. \\ \end{aligned} (3a)2+a29a2+a210a2a2aa​=32=9=9=109​=109​​=10​3​.​

How can you use this to find the side length of the colored square? The only triangle we haven't used yet is triangle C… could it help?

    The easiest way to solve this is to recognize that the small triangle is similar to the middle-sized triangle, with a similarity ratio of 1/3. That means (1/3)^2 represents the ratio between the areas of the small triangle to the middle-sized one, that is, 1 : 9.

    The area of the large triangle is the middle triangle plus an extra small one, which is 10/9 the area of the middle triangle.

    Four large triangles have a total area of (4)(1/2)(15)(5)=150, but that includes some overlap. What we really want is the area of four middle-sized triangles, which is (150)(9/10)=135.

    The big square (15x15) has area 225, and the slanted square has area 225 - 135 = 90.

    Figures are similar if they are the same shape. This means the lengths of their corresponding sides are proportional and their angles are equal.

    The legs of a right triangle are the two shorter sides.

    The Pythagorean theorem states that the squares on the legs of a right triangle add up in area to equal the square on the hypotenuse. Stated algebraically, this is a2+b2=c2a^2 + b^2 = c^2a2+b2=c2, where aaa and bbb are the lengths of the legs and ccc is the length of the hypotenuse.

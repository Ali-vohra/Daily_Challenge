To find the possible values of O, we need to find the possible values of L.

First off, none of our digits can be equal to 0 (since it's mentioned in the question that no number has leading zero)

Now, notice that in the right and left column, B+A+D and D+A+B are both equal to L, meaning that L can not be more than 9.

This indicates that O also can't be more than 9 because then, either A= 0 and that's impossible or if A>0, we will need to carry a digit to the left column which will increase the value of B+A+D and we don't want that.

So, what can L be?

It can't be 1, 2, 3,4 or 5 (remember B, A and D are different digits and any of these values will make at least two digits equal)

Changing focus to the middle column only and remembering that A can have only one value, we find that if,

â€¢ L=9,

A= 0 (not possible)

â€¢ L=8,

A =1 and 0 (definitely not possible cause that's two different values)

â€¢ L= 7,

A = 1 (possible)

â€¢ L = 6,

A = 1 (possible)

So, L is either 7 or 6 but A is always 1 since any other value will make O greater than 9.

If L = 6, B,A and D are 2,1 and 3 respectively (or the other way round), meaning that O = 8

If L= 7, B,A and D are 2,1 and 4 respectively (or the other way round), meaning O= 9

Therefore the only possible values for O are 8 or 9.

# prevalence = probability of disease in population P(D+)
# sensitivity = probability test identifies the disease P(T+|D+)
# specifity = probability test rules out the disease P(T-|D-)
# positive predictive value = probability of disease if test is positive P(D+|T+)

# Applying Bayes-Theorem:
def positive_predictive_value(prevalence, sensitivity, specifity):
   return (sensitivity * prevalence /
                        (prevalence * sensitivity + (1 - specifity)*(1 - prevalence)))

print(f"{positive_predictive_value(0.05, 0.94, 0.96):.2f}")

Bayes Theorem. I teach this to medical residents each summer. Sensitivity (true positives / all with disease - 94%) and specificity (true negatives / all without disease - 96%) are independent of prevalence (those with disease / entire population - 5%). But we donâ€™t know beforehand who does and does not have the disease. The patient does not care that the test identifies 94% of cases. They want to know â€œWhat is the chance I have the disease if my test is positiveâ€ (positive predictive value PPV, or â€œWhat is the chance I donâ€™t have the disease if my test is negativeâ€ (negative predictive value NPV)â€. PPV and NPV depend critically on prevalence. For situations where the prevalence is quite low, most positive tests are false positives. A good example is screening mammography. The prevalence of breast cancer in women 40 - 70 years old is about 0.6%. Sensitivity and specificity of mammograms are high, around 90%. But the PPV is only about 6%. 94% of positive mammograms are false positives in women who do not have breast cancer. Additional expensive or invasive tests (ultrasound, MR, biopsy) are needed to separate the false from the true positives.

This is classic Bayesian probability

Probability of Being infected when testing positive (conditional ) .....(3) =

Probability of Being infected and Testing positive ............(1)
/ Total Probability of testing positive = (Probability of Being infected and Testing positive + Probability of not being infected and testing positive..(2) )

(1) = Apriori probability of being infected * probability of testing positive when infected = .05*.94 = .047

(2) = Apriori probability of not being infected * probability of testing positive when uninfected = (1-.05)*(1-.96) = .95 * .04 = .038

(3) = .047 / .(047 + .038) = .047/.085 = 47/85 = 55%

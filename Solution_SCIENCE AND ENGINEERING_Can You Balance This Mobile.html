Can You Balance This Mobile?

Mobiles are dynamic sculptures that stay in perfect balance even as they twirl in the wind.

Balancing a mobile's decorative ornaments can be complicated. Mobiles often have multiple support rods that hang at different levels, adding layers of challenge. Can you balance the mobile in the question below?

A rod with hanging ornaments is perfectly balanced if it is supported at a particular point called its center of mass. This point is the average position of the masses hanging from the rod.

For example, suppose the ornament on the left is twice the weight of each of the other two ornaments. When the support is attached at the center of the rod, it tilts to the left. The red arrow shows the direction the rod begins to rotate. Where should you attach the string so that the rod is level and balanced?

The moment arms of both hangings must be equal, and moment arm (which generates the torque) is the weight, the force, multiplied by the distance to the fulcrum, in this case the wire, and in order for them to be equal you must have it at C, because there the moment arm of the left side hanging would be 6 (it's weight, six times the smaller ball) × 3 (there are three segments from C to the left side end, where the hanging is) which is 18 smaller ball weight/segment. As the force is counter clockwise, for simplicity will consider it to be "positive" (which simplifies the math) (This means that if the overall torque is zero the system will be balanced). Then we do the same for the right side weight, which has a weight of 9 (times the smaller ball's weight) and a distance from C of 2 segments so it is 2×9=18; in this case negative as it is clockwise (-18).

We then sum them and confirm the system is balanced as the overall torque is zero, 18 + (-18) = 0.

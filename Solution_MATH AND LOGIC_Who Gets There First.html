Who Gets There First?

You own a coffee shop, and your faithful friends Avery and Bruno come for a coffee every day. They like a little bit of routine but agree that too much routine is restrictive. On any given day, you can expect Avery to arrive at a random time in the range of 8:00 to 12:00, and Bruno at a random time somewhere from 6:00 to 10:00. Neither person's arrival time affects the other's.

What is the probability of Avery getting to the coffee shop first tomorrow? Keep reading to see one way we can approach this problem, or jump ahead for a slightly different challenge.

Let's see if a visual approach helps. Avery arrives sometime from 8:00 to 12:00, so we can think of her time as a random point somewhere on a line segment with endpoints labeled 8 and 12. Similarly, Bruno's arrival time can be a point somewhere on a segment with endpoints labeled 6 and 10.

Instead of having our segments overlap, let's position them perpendicularly as a set of axes. Now, we can think of a point in the square as a pair of arrival times by seeing where the point's coordinates fall on the axes. For example, the point (9, 10) relates to Avery arriving at 9:00 and Bruno arriving at 10:00.

Let's think about all of the points in the square relating to Avery arriving first. By connecting the points (8, 8) and (10, 10), we can draw a line dividing the square into two regions. The triangular region is made of all the points relating to Avery arriving first, while the other region relates to Bruno arriving first.

The area of the triangular region is 18\frac1881â€‹ the area of the entire square, so the probability that a point chosen randomly in the square is also in the triangle is 18.\frac18.81â€‹. Every randomly chosen point in the square relates to a random pair of arrival times for Avery and Bruno, so the probability Avery arrives first is equal to the probability of a randomly chosen point falling in the triangle, or 18.\frac18.81â€‹.

Can we adjust this approach to solve the challenge below, or is a different approach needed?

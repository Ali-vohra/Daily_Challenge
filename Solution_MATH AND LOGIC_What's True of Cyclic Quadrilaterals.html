What's True of Cyclic Quadrilaterals?

Although you might expect to find an exception to this rule, there are none: any triangle can be inscribed in a circle. The center of the circle will lie at the intersection of the perpendicular bisectors of the triangle's sides.

Can we do the same with any quadrilateral, though? Or is there something special about the quadrilaterals we can inscribe in a circle? Keep reading to find out, or jump ahead to today's challenge if you feel ready.

Let's start with a quadrilateral that's already inscribed in a circle and see if there's anything unusual about its measures. We'll start by looking at its angles. Instead of trying to find the exact measures of the angles, however, we'll just see if they're somehow related to one another.

Every angle of the quadrilateral is inscribed in the circle, and one thing we know about an inscribed angle is how it relates to the arc that subtends it: the arc's degree measure is twice the measure of the angle. Let's label a pair of opposite angle measures xºxºxº and yº,yº,yº, and use these measures to label their subtending arc measures:

While the measures of the angles don't seem directly related to each other, the arc measures are. The arcs combine to complete the circumference of the circle, so their degree measures must add up to 360º.360º.360º. We can conclude that

2x+2y=360x+y=180. \begin{aligned} 2x + 2y & = 360 \\ x + y & = 180.\\ \end{aligned} 2x+2yx+y​=360=180.​

We could use the same reasoning on the other pair of opposite angles of the quadrilateral to conclude the sum of their measures is also 180 º.\mathit{180}\,º.180º. Not every quadrilateral's opposite angles are related in this way, so cyclic quadrilaterals are indeed special!

A figured is said to be inscribed in another if it is drawn inside the other in such a way that they fit tightly and the inside figure just touches the outer figure. For example, a triangle is inscribed in another figure if all three of its vertices are on the boundary of the other figure.

A perpendicular bisector is a line that bisects another line (cuts it in half) and is also perpendicular to it (making right angles).

A quadrilateral is a four-sided polygon.

An arc of a circle is a connected part of the circle's outer edge.

An object subtends an angle if the rays that extend from the vertex of the angle touch the edges of the object.

The circumference of a circle is the distance around the outside of the circle.

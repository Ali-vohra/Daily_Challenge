Before the Light Turns Red

Life is a fast-paced game. Cars, buses, trains, and people are on the move from morning through the night. It’s a lot to keep track of.

But from the perspective of physics, tracking a moving object requires only a few pieces of information: its position and velocity at one moment in time, as well as its acceleration. This is the power of kinematics.

In principle, this information allows us to predict the position and speed of an object at any future time. But because the acceleration of an object is rarely constant, this can be a tricky business — the tools of calculus may be required.

However, we have some ways to simplify the problem.

Suppose a motorcycle is accelerating onto a highway. It starts at a low speed and approaches a higher speed.

This is plotted on the graph below. You can see that the motorcycle’s speed changes more rapidly at the beginning of the time we’re tracking it, and it doesn’t change as rapidly once it reaches top speed.

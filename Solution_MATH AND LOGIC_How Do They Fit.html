  • [1] J is in a row below X's row.
  • [2] F is farther east than V.
  • [3] E touches Z (they can touch diagonally).

Because [1] the letter J is in a row below X's row, piece 1 (seen below) must be up against the top of the grid, and piece 2 must be against the bottom of the grid. If piece 1 were placed on the right, then the letter V would be furthest east, and so [2] F could not be further east than V - so piece 1 must be placed on the left. Piece 3 can be placed in only one way to satisfy rules [2] and [3].

J is at position C4.

Rearrange the Squares

If we added the first six terms of this sequence

1,1,4,9,25,64,…, 1, 1, 4, 9, 25, 64, \ldots, 1,1,4,9,25,64,…,

we would get 1+1+4+9+25+64=104.1 + 1 + 4 + 9 + 25 + 64 = 104.1+1+4+9+25+64=104.

To find the sum of the first seven, eight, or more terms, one approach would be to find the additional terms and then manually add them to the sum we already have. Is there a quicker way? Keep reading to find out, or jump to the challenge if you feel ready.

Looking at the sequence again, we can see that each term is a perfect square:

1,1,4,9,25,64,…12,12,22,32,52,82,… \def\arraystretch{1.5} \begin{array}{lllllll} 1, & 1, & 4, & 9, & 25, & 64, & \ldots \\ 1^2, & 1^2, & 2^2, & 3^2, & 5^2, & 8^2, & \ldots \\ \end{array}1,12,​1,12,​4,22,​9,32,​25,52,​64,82,​……​

Each number that gets squared is the sum of the previous two. Our sequence is made by squaring the terms of the Fibonacci sequence. That lets us predict the next few terms in the sequence:

12,12,22,32,52,82,132,212,…1,1,4,9,25,64,169,441,…, \def\arraystretch{1.5} \begin{array}{lllllll} 1^2, & 1^2, & 2^2, & 3^2, & 5^2, & 8^2, & 13^2, & 21^2, & \ldots \\ 1, & 1, & 4, & 9, & 25, & 64, & 169, & 441, & \ldots, \\ \end{array}12,1,​12,1,​22,4,​32,9,​52,25,​82,64,​132,169,​212,441,​……,​

but adding them up is going to get more and more tedious the farther we go. Let's see if we can reason our way to a shortcut.

Every term of our sequence is a square, so why not represent them geometrically? The sum 1+1+4+9+25+64=1041 + 1 + 4 + 9 + 25 + 64 = 1041+1+4+9+25+64=104 we saw at the beginning is the total area of these squares:

Rearranging the squares won't change their total area if we don't make them overlap. We can stack the first two squares to make a rectangle exactly as wide as the third square:

whose height is the same as the fourth square's:

In fact, we can keep forming rectangles like this for as long as we want, since every square's side length is the sum of the previous two:

Does this give us a quicker way to find the total area than adding up all the individual areas? How might it help if we want the sum of even more terms of our sequence?

    a,b,sum=0,1,0
    while(n>0):
        b1=b*b
        sum=sum+b1
        a,b=b,a+b
        n=n-1
    return sum

The answer is: 1870.

Generally:
Fibonacci: f(1) = 1, f(2) = 1, f(i > 2) = f(i - 2) + f(i - 1).
Fibonacci Squared: fs(i) = f(i) * f(i).
Sum of Fibonacci Squared: sfs(i) = f(i) * f(i + 1).
* Note: fs() is not needed for sfs().

Specifically:
sfs(9) = f(9) * f(10) = 34 * 55 = 1870.

On the RHS, we can multiply the N2 term by 3 and the AlCl3 by 2. If we multiply the NH4CL04 term by 6, we will have H and O as 24. H appears only in the H2O term. Therefore, we can balance it by multiplying the H2O term by 12. But this gives us the value of O on RHS = 12 + a*Al2O3. So 3a + 12 = 24. Therefore a=4

We get yAl + 6NH4CLO4 = 4Al2O3 + 2AlCl3 + 3N2 + 12H2O with y to be determined. On the RHS, we get the weight of Al as 4*2 + 2 = 10

So if we denote the molecular weight of Al as x, the molecular weight of NH4CLO4 will be 4x. Therefore , percentage weight by mass of Al in the propellant = 10x / (10x + 24x) = 10/24 * 100 = 29% approximately

    If I slice the cubes into slabs, each adjacent pair of slabs have the same number of shaded and unshaded cubes.

    If I consider one slab, each pair of rows has the same number of shaded and unshaded cubes.

    If I consider a row, each pair of cubes cancel each other out.

    So, without counting how many cubes are in each cube, I can remove...

    ...equal numbers of shaded and unshaded by removing pairs of slabs until there is just one slab left...

    ...then pairs of rows until then here is one row left...

    ...then pairs of cubes until there is only one cube left. That cube will always be shaded.

    So, each cube has one more shaded than unshaded cube. Since the cubes on the 3x3x3 cube are bigger, that has more of its volume shaded.

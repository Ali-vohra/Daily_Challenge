Not a Whole Circle

The figure below is made from a square, an equilateral triangle, and three semicircles. One semicircle (blue) has an area of 3.3.3.

What are the areas of the other two semicircles? Keep reading to find out, or skip ahead to today's challenge.

We could use the area of the blue semicircle to find its diameter, which forms an altitude of the equilateral triangle. Maybe then we could find the equilateral triangle's side length. That would give us the square's side length, which would let us go to work on finding the other semicircles' areas.

That might get a little messy, so let's think of a different approach. Instead of directly solving for these lengths, let's just see how they're related. Maybe then we can relate the diameters of the semicircles, and then relate their areas.

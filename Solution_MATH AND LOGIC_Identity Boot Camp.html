The second equation show us that q + s = 0 + r + t + q + 2q + 3q/6. If you multiply the two sides by 6, you can see that q disappear and that s = r + t/6, that is 7 + 5/6 = 2. Therefore, s = 2. And the third equation, finally, is the final piece of the puzzle to solve the question. It said that q + 0/2 = q x t/ 10. If you multiplies the two sides of the equation by 2, we can see that q = 2qt/10, what is the same that qt/5. So, 5q = qt, and we cut the q again, resting the 5. And we can conclude that t = 5. So, the sum of r + s + t = 14. I hope you enjoyed my solution and any mistake you can fix on the comments.
